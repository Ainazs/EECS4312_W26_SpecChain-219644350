# Application: [MindDoc] 
- Most important difference between pipelines: [The manual pipeline made personas that were more focused and based on specific user situations found in the reviews. The automated pipeline made personas that were more general and sometimes didn't match their source group. The automated persona for the questionnaire group, for instance, ended up with pricing requirements instead of check-in ones.]
- Most useful pipeline: [The hybrid pipeline gave the most balanced results because it kept the speed of automation while using human judgment to fix personas that weren't aligned, rewrite vague requirements, and add missing requirements like language support that the automated pipeline missed completely.] 
- Most surprising finding: [Some automated requirements were grammatically correct but too vague to make sure that tests could be reliably generated.]
- Observed weakness in the automated pipeline: [Some personas had unsupported assumptions that weren't based on the reviews. The language support group's automated persona didn't say anything about language at all, and the updates group's persona focused on general frustration instead of the privacy issues that were most important to that group's reviews.]
